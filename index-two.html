SpaceJack

HOW TO PLAY?

OBJECT OF THE GAME

A Human can save the Earth by collecting 10 magic stars. To do so, he shall attempt to beat Alien in SpaceJack by getting a count as close to 21 as possible, without going over 21.

WINS AND LOSES

The winner is a player whose total value of cards is closer or equal 21. After each game the Human will: receive 1 star if won or lose 1 star if defeated. Human have 5 stars in the beginning of the game. The goal is to collect 10 stars in total.

CARD VALUES

Face cards have a value of 10 and any other card is its number value. It is up to each individual player if an ace is worth 1 or 11.

THE HUMAN'S PLAY

The Human goes first and must decide whether to "check" (end the set by comparing the results with Alien's score) or "draw" (ask for another card in an attempt to get closer to a count of 21, or even hit 21 exactly).

THE ALIEN'S PLAY

When the Alien has served cards for a Human, his second card is face-down. If the total value of Alien's cards is 17 or more, it must stand. If the total is 16 or under, he must take a card. Alien must continue to take cards until the total is 17 or more.

OUR MISSION

This SpaceJack game is based on the rules of the popular casino game: Blackjack. The layout and rules are simplified to a child level. The goal is to make learning of first math actions fun.

The game is taking your child on the journey to space with a very special assignment to save the Earth. The value of the cards is shown below but the sum is hidden so your child has to sum it in order to make a right decision between ending a game (check) or adding another card in case the sum is too small.
