SpaceJack

HOW TO PLAY?

OBJECT OF THE GAME

A Human can save the Earth by collecting 10 magic stars. To do so, he shall attempt to beat Alien in SpaceJack by getting a count as close to 21 as possible, without going over 21. The value of the cards will appear next to the Player name once set is over.


WINS AND LOSES

The winner is a player whose total value of cards is closer or equal 21. If an Alien goes over 21, he automatically loses. After each game the Human will: receive 1 star if won or lose 1 star if defeated. Humans have 5 stars in the beginning of the game. The goal is to collect 10 stars in total. Humans can see the amount of stars below his name.


CARD VALUES

Face cards have a value of 10, ace has value of 11 and any other card has a number value as shown of the card.



THE HUMAN'S PLAY

The Human goes first and must decide. Option one is: "Draw" - ask for another card in an attempt to get closer to a count of 21, or even hit 21 exactly. Option two is: "Check" - see Aliens cards and compare results. Watch out! Human have only 10 seconds to check!


THE ALIEN'S PLAY

When the Alien has served cards for a Human, his second card is face-down. If the total value of Alien's cards is 17 or more, it must stand. If the total is 16 or under, he must take a card. Alien can take only one card.

OUR MISSION

Get ready for a journey to space with a very special assignment to save the Earth!

Welcome to SpaceJack - a fun way to practice basic math, memory and decision making skills! The layout and rules are simplified to a child level (4+) while adults can also enjoy the game.

SpaceJack is based on the rules of the popular casino game: Blackjack. Although some may believe that this old card game is just a gambling practice - in reality there is a full range of skills this game can teach us:

Patience: In general card games teach us to remain unflappable and calm. Mastering this skill is extremely important these days as society becomes more and more impatient. Undoubtful, learning how to use strategy planning will be useful in school! :)

Concentration: Another worth practicing skill, closely connected with patience. In the world of distractions, being able to remember and recall information is crucial. This is why the Player has to remember the value of the cards and correctly sum them in order to make a decision about the next step, which leads us to...

Decision making: Presented with so much information and enormous range of choices, the decision making skills are very important. In this game, the decision making process is essential. There is also a "luck" factor, as the cards are random. It is a good lesson of life flow, where our control over the universe is limited. Sometimes we win and sometimes we lose. It is important for a child to learn how to lose and understand that this is just another part of life.
